Play Games for a Better World

Many video games have long been associated with violence. But are all of them evil? Can we play games for good cause?

Higgs Valley, a team of game developers in Vietnam, is determined to turn around the negative perception of games with their latest product, "Soft Wings."

Hung Duong, the team leader, shares that the game is inspired by the continuing territorial and maritime disputes in Asia-Pacific as well as tensions in other parts of the world.

“We have been witnessing unprecedented crises around the world. Wars, conflicts, diseases, etc. are threatening peace, stability and prosperity of humankind. Even in the East, where peace has been a long held value, tension is elevating. Yet not all of us are aware of what is going on around us. We wonder if it is our ignorance that leads to chaos."

As game developers, Higgs Valley would like to send a wake up call to the world. The game is simple yet challenging; players navigate a paper airplane flying above a sea of battleships and choppers. But the message is powerful; no fire, no killing, no violence, yet you can feel the vulnerability in your hands.

“That’s all that we want you to feel!," says Hung.

Enjoy the game!
